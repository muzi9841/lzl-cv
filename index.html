  • PM2.5 Induced Pulmonary Fibrosis in Vivo and in Vitro 10/2016-06/2018

    Shanghai Hongqiao International Medical Research Institution, Adviser: Xiaobei Deng
        Investigated the potential mechanism by which PM2.5 exposure adversely induced PF in vivo and in vitro, 6-week-old C57/BL6J mice were intranasally administrated with PM2.5 (100 μg/day) for 4 weeks;
        Concluded that PM2.5 could induce PF by targeting pulmonary epithelium, macrophages, and fibroblasts, suggesting that PM2.5 was a potent initiator of PF;
        This project earned honors such as the "Exceptional Design Award" of the 3rd "Yifanqihang" Undergraduate Innovative Practical Competition, the municipal-level project of Shanghai.
